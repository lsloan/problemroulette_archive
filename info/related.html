E2Coach

  • About E2Coach

    The Michigan Tailoring System is currently being used for a variety of applications in public health-everything from cessation of smoking to losing weight. We are working to adapt MTS with the goal of helping physics students improve their performance in introductory courses. We call this "E2Coach", E2 for electronic and expert. E2Coach provides each student with an individualized coach, one that will recognize their strengths and weaknesses, understand their motivations, and help them succeed in the course.

    E2Coach is unique in that it aims to help all students-the student at risk of failure as well as an A student. Its sole purpose is to be on the students' side, providing useful tools tailored to their individual needs.

  • E2Coach & Problem Roulette

    In analysis of Problem Roulette (PR) usage statistics, we have used data collected from E2Coach, such as incoming GPA to assess the influence of selection bias on the grade performance of regular PR users.

    PR is an option for E2Coach to recommend that students use to improve their class performance


1-Bit Matrix Completion

  • About 1-Bit Matrix Completion

    This is a theory of matrix completion for the extreme case of noisy 1-bit observations. Instead of observing a subset of the real-valued entries of a matrix M, we obtain a small number of binary (1-bit) measurements generated according to a probability distribution determined by the real-valued entries of M. The goal is to obtain an accurate estimate of M from this data.

  • 1-Bit Matrix Completion & Problem Roulette

    The dataset gathered from Problem Roulette (PR) usage statistics forms a large 1-bit (problem answered correctly or incorrectly) matrix partially filled with student-problem instances. This matrix serves as a suitable testing ground for the matrix completion algorithm.

    A possible future development of PR could include this matrix completion algorithm in order to direct students to a certain type of problem. For example, one could direct a student to problems in an area in which he/she is weak.
